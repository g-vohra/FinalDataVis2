Australia’s CO2 Emissions

The Visualisation dwells deep into CO2 emissions in Australia, depicting changes over the years and the impact of energy consumption. The visualizations below depict trends in CO2 emissions, natural resource consumption, and more, offering a comprehensive look at environmental data crucial for policy-making and public awareness.

CO2 Emissions Reduced Over Time

The bubble chart illustrates the reduction of CO2 emissions over time due to various emission reduction techniques employed by each state. It is clear that Australian states have been effectively tackling the issue of co2 emissions and preserving our environment.

Natural Resource Consumption Organisations In Australia

Loading Map...

This map highlights the distribution of organizations focused on natural resource consumption across Australia. These organisations can be regarded as the main natural resource consumption organisations in Australia, leading to higher CO2 emissions.

Total CO2 Emissions Vs Reduced Emissions by State

This bar chart compares total CO2 emissions against reduced emissions in Australian states, helping to identify where efforts have been most effective. It is clear that Victoria has been very effective in tackling the issue of CO2 emissions, however the other states are producing significantly more CO2, then they are reducing.

Annual Change In IP Emissions In Australia

The line chart tracks annual changes in industrial process emissions (IP emissions) in Australia, providing a visual representation of trends and fluctuations over the years. You can use the brushing feature of the bottom line chart to highlight the specific year you are interested in analysing.

CO2 Intensity Vs Energy Intensity In Australia

The line chart demonstrates the relationship between CO2 intensity and energy intensity, showing the overall relationship of energy use with CO2 emissions.

CO2 Emissions by Year In Australian States

This area line chart provides a year-by-year breakdown of CO2 emissions for each Australian state, helping to track state-specific trends and changes over time. It is clear that the Northern Territory has been significantly contributing to high C02 emissions, whereas Victoria has left a low carbon footprint.
